🏠 Home Preparation

Make your home safe and welcoming for your new kitten

🚫 Cat-Proofing Checklist

  • Remove toxic plants (lilies, azaleas, etc.)
  • Secure loose electrical wires
  • Remove small objects (rubber bands, etc.)
  • Install cabinet locks if needed
  • Check for holes/gaps kitten could get stuck in
  • Secure balcony/windows with screens

🛏️ Safe Room Setup

  • Choose quiet room away from main traffic
  • Place food and water bowls
  • Set up litter box (away from food)
  • Add comfortable bedding/hiding spots
  • Include scratching post
  • Add some toys for stimulation

🏥 Veterinary Care Plan

Organize your kitten's healthcare needs

👩‍⚕️ Veterinarian Information

ℹ️
First Visit Reminder: Schedule your kitten's first vet visit within 3-5 days of adoption, even if they seem healthy. This establishes care and catches any issues early.

📋 First Visit Checklist

  • Bring adoption papers/medical records
  • List any concerns or questions
  • Bring stool sample if possible
  • Discuss vaccination schedule
  • Ask about spay/neuter timing
  • Discuss diet and feeding schedule

💉 Typical Vaccination Schedule

  • 📅 6-8 weeks: First FVRCP
  • 📅 10-12 weeks: Second FVRCP
  • 📅 14-16 weeks: Third FVRCP + Rabies
  • 📅 1 year: Booster shots
  • 📅 Annual: Yearly check-ups
  • ⚠️ Schedule may vary based on kitten's health and vet recommendations

⏰ Daily Schedule & Routine

Establish a healthy routine for your kitten

🍽️ Feeding Schedule

🌅 Morning Routine

  • 🍽️ First meal of the day
  • 🧹 Clean litter box
  • 🎮 Interactive play session
  • 🫂 Gentle socializing time

🌆 Evening Routine

  • 🍽️ Final meal of the day
  • 🎯 Energy-burning play time
  • ✂️ Grooming session
  • 🛏️ Prepare sleeping area
💡
Routine Benefits: Consistent schedules help kittens feel secure and make house training easier. Adjust timing to fit your lifestyle, but try to keep meal times consistent.

👥 Family & Pet Introductions

Plan safe introductions to family members and other pets

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Household Members

🐕🐱 Other Pets

⚠️
Introduction Safety: Always supervise initial meetings between your kitten and other pets or young children. Go slow and watch for stress signals from all animals involved.

🐕 Introducing to Dogs

  • 🏠 Keep kitten in safe room initially
  • 👃 Let them smell each other's scent first
  • 👀 Visual contact through baby gate
  • 🍽️ Feed on opposite sides of door
  • ⏰ Short, supervised meetings
  • 🎁 Reward calm behavior with treats

🐱 Introducing to Other Cats

  • 🚪 Separate rooms for 1-2 weeks
  • 🔄 Switch sleeping blankets daily
  • 🍽️ Feed near the door (but separated)
  • 🎮 Interactive play sessions
  • 👁️ Brief visual contact sessions
  • ⚖️ Gradual increase in time together

🚨 Emergency Preparedness

Be prepared for any health or safety emergencies

📞 Emergency Contacts

🏥 Emergency Kit Essentials

  • 🩹 First aid supplies
  • 🌡️ Digital thermometer (normal: 100.4-102.5°F)
  • 💊 Any prescribed medications
  • 🧤 Disposable gloves
  • 📋 Emergency contact list
  • 🚗 Secure cat carrier

⚠️ Emergency Warning Signs

  • 🚫 Not eating for 24+ hours
  • 💨 Difficulty breathing
  • 🤢 Repeated vomiting
  • 🩸 Blood in stool or urine
  • 😵 Lethargy or unresponsiveness
  • 🌡️ Fever over 103°F
  • 💧 Straining to urinate
  • 🦴 Suspected trauma/injury
🚨
When in Doubt: If you're unsure whether something is an emergency, call your vet or emergency clinic. It's always better to be safe when it comes to your kitten's health.
