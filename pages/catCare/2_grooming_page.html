✂️ Grooming

Keep your kitten healthy and comfortable with proper grooming practices

💡
Gentle Approach: Grooming should be carried out with as little restraint as possible to keep your kitten comfortable and stress-free.

🪶 Brushing & Coat Care

Use a brush or a grooming mitt up to once a week to keep your kitten's coat healthy and reduce shedding. Regular brushing helps prevent matting and allows you to bond with your kitten.

⚠️
Watch for Stress Signals: If you notice your cat twitching, swishing their tail, growling or hissing, end the session immediately. These are signs your kitten is uncomfortable and needs a break.

💅 Claw Care

  • 🌳 Scratching posts help keep claws naturally trimmed
  • 🏃‍♂️ Being active outside and climbing trees maintains claw health
  • ✂️ Occasionally claws may need to be trimmed
  • 🏠 Trimming can be done at home with proper tools
  • 🏥 Your vet can also trim claws during visits
  • 📖 Ask your vet to show you the proper technique first

🛁 Bathing Guidelines

  • 🚫 Cats typically do not need to be bathed
  • ☠️ Only bathe if there's something toxic on their fur
  • 🧽 A damp cloth can remove most messes
  • 💧 Use the cloth method when possible
  • 🧴 If bathing is necessary, use cat-specific shampoo
  • 🌡️ Ensure water is lukewarm, never hot

📋 Grooming Best Practices

🎯 Step-by-Step Approach

  • 🕐 Choose a quiet time when your kitten is relaxed
  • 🤗 Start with gentle pets to get them comfortable
  • 🪶 Begin brushing with light, gentle strokes
  • 👀 Watch your kitten's body language constantly
  • ⏰ Keep sessions short, especially when starting
  • 🎁 Reward with treats or play after grooming
  • 📅 Build up to longer sessions gradually
  • ✋ Always stop if your kitten shows stress signals
✅
Remember: Most cats are excellent self-groomers! Your role is to provide gentle assistance and check for any issues during bonding time. Less is often more when it comes to grooming.
