Essential socialisation experiences for your kitten's development

On socialising your kitten, they should experience: different people, handling (including elements of vet handling, such as looking in their ears and handling their paws), sounds, and toys.

üë• Different People

Let the kitten meet different types of people. If the kitten is nervous, limit to one at a time in their safe space.

ü§≤ Handling

Handle the kitten frequently, including the following:

  • Restrained in hand
  • Holding head
  • Looking in ears
  • Examining neck
  • Stroking head, neck, back and tail
  • Touching paws and legs
  • Open mouth
  • Groom with soft brush

Stop if cat shows any sign of distress.

ü¶∂ Surfaces

Make sure the kitten walks on different surfaces, including:

  • Shiny surfaces
  • Tiles
  • Lino
  • Carpet
  • Rough surfaces

üß≥ Cat Carrier

  • Let the kitten find treats in their cat carrier while you are present in the room
  • Once the cat has spent a few minutes in the carrier, practise closing the door
  • Next try lifting the carrier, potentially with a blanket draped over
  • Take brief car journeys that result in a reward at home
‚ö†Ô∏è
Start the process again if kitten shows signs of distress.

üîä Sounds

Sound socialisation tends to be done while kittens are in their foster homes. However, continuing sound socialisation may be beneficial after their socialisation period.

  1. Test the sound volume when the kittens are not in the room, so as not to spook them. Set the volume to a very low level so that it is barely audible.
  2. Start playing the audio at your marked low volume.
  3. If there are no adverse reactions from the kitten with the sound at the current level then you can leave the sounds playing through.
  4. You can systematically increase the volume depending on the reaction of the kitten.
