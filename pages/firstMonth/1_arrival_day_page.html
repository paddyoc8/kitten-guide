🏠 Arrival Day

Step-by-step guide for your kitten's first day home

← Planning & Prep Next: Training & Care →

📋 Before Pickup

Complete these preparations before leaving to collect your kitten

• Ensure water bowl and litter box are prepared in safe room
• Line carrier with absorbent towel or newspaper
• Have familiar blanket or towel ready to drape over carrier
• Secure carrier in a stable position in the car and ensure drive is smooth
💡
Important: Keep the car ride as calm and quiet as possible. Speak softly to your kitten during transport and avoid sudden movements or loud noises.

🕐 Your Kitten's First Day Timeline

Follow these steps for a successful transition to their new home

Step 1: Arrival (First 30 minutes)

🏠 Safe Room Introduction

Bring carrier directly to safe room. Place carrier on floor and open door. Sit quietly nearby and speak in calm, soft voices. Don't reach for kitten or try to coax them out.

Step 2: Let Them Emerge

🐾 Natural Exploration

When kitten comes out, continue speaking calmly but don't reach for them. If they approach, offer hand to sniff. If they flinch, stay still and hold back for a bit.

Step 3: Initial Observation (15-30 minutes)

👀 Gentle Monitoring

Watch them explore their new space. You want to be there long enough for them to emerge and get a sense of you, but not so long your presence becomes stressful.

Shorten to 10-15 minutes if you see: panting, excessive vocalization, or staying completely motionless

Step 4: Decompression Time

😌 Alone Time

Give 2-3 hours of completely alone time. This lets them explore, find litter box and food, and start to relax without being observed.

Step 5: Visiting

🍽️ Feeding Time Visits

Do 15-20 minute visits throughout the day allowing for decompression time in between. Coincide visits with feeding times and sit quietly in the room while kitten is eating.

Step 6: Overnight

🌙 First Night

Leave the kitten to sleep overnight in the safe room. This gives them uninterrupted time to settle and feel secure in their new environment.

⚠️
Remember: Every kitten adjusts differently. Some may be confident and exploratory, while others might hide for several hours or even days. Both responses are completely normal. Patience is key!
