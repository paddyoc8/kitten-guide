🤔 What's Normal?

Understanding Cat Behaviors

Learn what's normal, what needs attention, and what requires action

✅ Normal Behaviors (Don't Worry!)

😸 Everyday Normal Behaviors

  • Kneading: Pushing paws into soft surfaces while purring - shows contentment
  • Head butting & cheek rubbing: Marking territory and showing affection
  • Chattering at birds: Natural hunting instinct when watching prey
  • Zoomies: Sudden bursts of running/jumping, especially after litter box
  • Bringing "gifts": Toys or objects presented proudly with meowing
  • Sleeping 12-16 hours daily: Natural cat sleep pattern

⚠️ Monitor & Address

👀 Behaviors to Watch & Redirect

  • Excessive furniture scratching: Provide better alternatives, redirect behavior
  • Litter box avoidance: Check cleanliness, location; consult vet if persists
  • Excessive vocalization: May indicate pain, anxiety, or attention-seeking
  • Aggressive play biting: Redirect to toys, never use hands for play
  • Extended hiding: May indicate illness, stress, or fear

🚨 Concerning - Vet Needed

🚨
Emergency Alert: These behaviors require immediate veterinary attention. Don't wait - contact your vet or emergency clinic right away.

🩺 Immediate Vet Attention Required

  • Straining in litter box: Potential blockage - immediate vet attention
  • Sudden appetite loss: No eating for 24+ hours needs prompt vet care
  • Lethargy with other symptoms: Combined with vomiting, diarrhea, breathing changes
  • Breathing difficulties: Open-mouth breathing, rapid breathing at rest
  • Excessive grooming: Creating bald patches or skin irritation

💙 Positive Bonding Signs

🥰 Signs Your Cat Loves You

  • Slow blinking: "Cat kisses" - return the slow blink!
  • Following you around: Shows attachment and companionship desire
  • Exposing belly: Ultimate sign of trust (admire, don't always touch!)
  • Purring when touched: Shows contentment and enjoyment
  • Grooming you: Treating you as family - high honor!

📖 Quick Body Language Guide

😊 Relaxed/Happy:
  • Ears forward
  • Tail up or gently moving
  • Slow blinking
  • Normal posture
😰 Stressed/Fearful:
  • Ears back or flattened
  • Low body posture
  • Hiding or freezing
  • Dilated pupils
😡 Aggressive/Angry:
  • Arched back
  • Puffed fur
  • Hissing or growling
  • Swatting motions
🔍 Alert/Interested:
  • Ears perked forward
  • Tail still or twitching tip
  • Focused gaze
  • Slightly crouched
💡
Remember: Every cat is unique! These are general guidelines. When in doubt about your cat's behavior, always consult with your veterinarian for personalized advice.
